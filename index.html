Assessing Website Validity

Fake news is a hot topic these days. The spread of misinformation has become so prevalent that countries around the world have even begun to legislate against it.

On the internet, anyone can be a publisher but not everyone knows how to properly vet the information they consume. Whether false or misleading information was created maliciously or unintentionally, individuals need to know how to discern the trustworthiness of the content they consume.

The high level goal of this lesson is to give you the tools you need to be able to discriminate between quality content and false information.

Specifically, by the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Identify all four components of the CARS checklist
  • Explain how each component is used to evaluate the validity of a website.

The CARS Checklist (Credibility, Accuracy, Reasonableness, Support) is a framework which helps us gauge the validity of the websites we browse. It’s important to realize that even credible websites with high quality content may not meet every criteria of this checklist. Though unlikely, some disreputable sites might even meet every list item. Even while utilizing this checklist, we should always be consuming content thoughtfully!

Credibility

Trustworthiness of the source and the sources references; High quality evidence; Reasonable, logically-sound arguments; Author has credentials or experience; Peer-reviewed or evidence of quality control; Author or organization is well-known and respected.

Goal: an authoritative source that provides solid, trustworthy evidence.

Accuracy

Information is: up-to-date, factual, detailed, exact, comprehensive; Evidence that the source has intentions of completeness and accuracy.

Goal: a source that provides content which is up-to-date and tries to give a complete picture.

Reasonableness

Information is: fair, balanced, objective, reasoned; There is no conflict of interest; There is an absence of fallacies or slanted tone.

Goal: a source that provides thoughtful, reasonable and truthful content.

Support

Credible references listed; Valid contact information; Content can be corroborated; Claims supported; Valid documentation is provided.
